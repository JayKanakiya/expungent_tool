Expungement gives you a clean slate. Having criminal charges on your record can prevent you from finding employment, public housing, and other government services. Expunging your criminal record can make accessing work, housing, and services easier. Generally, it provides a stronger level of protection and in some cases is as if the record never existed.

Expungement Tools can break down the process into more user-friendly chunks, telling visitors, for example, how to get a copy of their actual criminal records. They can provide a series of simply worded questions to help users discover whether charges filed against them are potentially expungeable, including queries about how old they were when charged, where the criminal case occurred and whether the individual was released without conviction. In addition, users are potentially able to download expungement petitions and fee waivers directly from the website. An example of one of these tools is ExpungeMaryland.org. The Expunge Maryland tool is user facing meaning the individual seeking to have their record expunged is who the tool is designed for. The individual will answer a series of questions surrounding their circumstances and then based on the answers a recommendation will be whether or not the individual’s record is eligible for expungement along with next possible steps.

Record sealing is the legal process of concealing or hiding charges from a person’s permanent criminal record so that it is not accessible by the public, though it might still be available to law enforcement and certain prospective employers. Once a person’s records are sealed, all of the proceedings recounted in a person’s record are treated as if they never occurred.

Getting a criminal record sealed or expunged is not easy in any state. Most people have no idea which charges can be expunged or how the process works. While the Internet is chock-full of information, it's often of little help to most because the information is difficult to decipher and involves lengthy explanations of a complicated state statute. Information can be found using “Expungement Tools” which are web-based interactive apps providing general information and access to state specific links, or attorney websites.
